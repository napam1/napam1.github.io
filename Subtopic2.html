App that Translates Speech to Sign Language
Site Author
 Jasmin Sykes


Abstract
 This article shows an app called Terp 360, an app that uses AI-powered 3D avatars to translate speech into sign language. The app functions like a Google Translate for sign language, providing real-time signed translations from speech or text without needing a human interpreter. Savatia created the app in response to the communication barriers Deaf people in Kenya face, especially in workplaces, schools, and healthcare settings where staff typically do not know sign language. Terp 360 was developed with Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing Kenyans and features over 2,300 recorded signs collected using motion sensors. It currently supports translations from English and Swahili into Kenyan Sign Language, and developers plan to expand it to include multiple African and global sign languages.

 iHealthAssist
Site Author

Abstract
 This site shows another tool called iHealthAssist, an AI-powered avatar concierge system designed to improve patient experience and accessibility in healthcare settings. It’s a digital workforce of animated avatars that help hospitals enhance patient experiences without requiring additional staff. This site provides information about this concierge system, describing its aspects and how these avatars are completely customizable by being able to change the background, style, and personality. It also explains how this system can be brandable, leading to changing the personality to someone polished or playful for any business needs. This system isn’t only focused on American Sign Language but also has over 150 languages to choose from.
